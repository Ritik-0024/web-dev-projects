Stay connected and check up on them.

Staying connected and regularly checking up on someone dealing with mental health challenges is crucial for providing ongoing support and showing that you care. Here’s how to effectively maintain this connection:

Reinforces Support

Regular check-ins remind them that they’re not alone and that you’re consistently available for support. Frequent contact can help maintain an open line of communication, making it easier for them to reach out when needed.

Monitors Progress

Regular interactions allow you to monitor any changes in their mental health and adjust your support accordingly. Staying connected helps you notice early signs if their condition worsens, allowing you to offer help or encourage them to seek professional support.

Strengthens the Relationship

Consistent check-ins foster trust and deepen your relationship, showing that you genuinely care about their well-being. Regular contact helps preserve and strengthen the bond, even if they’re not always responsive or open.

Be Genuine and Supportive

Show genuine concern and interest in their well-being. Simple messages like “Just checking in to see how you’re doing” can go a long way. Let them know you’re available to help with specific tasks or needs, such as running errands or providing emotional support.

Engage in Shared Activities

Suggest activities that you can do together, such as a walk, a meal, or a hobby. Shared experiences can provide positive interactions and strengthen your connection. Choose activities that they’re comfortable with and that align with their current state of mind and energy levels.


Staying connected and checking up on someone with mental health challenges involves a balance of regular contact, empathy, and respect for their boundaries. By being a consistent and supportive presence, you can help them feel valued and understood, and provide meaningful support throughout their mental health journey.
